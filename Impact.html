AI is already around us

AI appears in phones, cars, hospitals, schools, banks, search engines and creative tools.

How AI changes daily life

AI has become part of everyday life. Search engines use AI to rank information, social media platforms recommend content, mobile phones organise photos, and online shops suggest products. In education, AI tools can explain topics and support revision. In business, AI can help analyse sales, answer customer questions and reduce repetitive tasks.

The impact is not only technical. It is also social. AI changes how people work, how students learn and how organisations make decisions. For this reason, people must understand both the benefits and the risks.

Three major social effects

  1. Workforce change: Routine tasks can be automated, so workers need digital skills.
  2. Decision support: AI can analyse data quickly, but final decisions should be checked by people.
  3. Ethical pressure: Organisations must protect privacy, fairness and safety.
